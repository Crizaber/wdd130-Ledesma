Quick Practice Projects

Now that you've learned the basics of crochet, it's time to practice with some small, beginner-friendly projects! These are simple patterns designed to help you build confidence while still making something fun and useful.

Core Skills Learned

These three projects help you practice the most important skills in crochet:

  1. Working in loops and rounds
  2. Increasing (INC) stitches
  3. Decreasing (DEC) stitches
  4. Changing yarn colors
  5. Finishing your work neatly

What's Next?

Once you're comfortable with these basics, you'll be ready for bigger projects like:

  • Detailed Plushies
  • Cozy Blankets
  • Custom Clothing

1. Granny Square: Rd 1

MR, CH 3, 2 DC, CH 2
(3 DC, CH 2) x3
SL ST to close

1. Granny Square: Rd 2

In each corner: 3 DC, CH 2, 3 DC
SL ST to finish

Tip: Use a new color here if you want!

1. Granny Square: Rd 3

Repeat Round 2, switching colors every round if desired.

Tip: Changing colors each round creates a striped, patterned effect!

2. Amigurumi Ball: Setup

Round 1: MR, SC 6

Round 2: INC around (12)

Round 3: (SC, INC) x6 (18)

2. Amigurumi Ball: Body

Round 4-5: SC around (18)

Round 6: (SC, DEC) x6 (12)

Stuff lightly with filling before moving forward!

2. Amigurumi Ball: Finish

Round 7: DEC around (6)

Close and weave in ends.

Tip: You can turn this into a keychain, bead, or tiny plush head!

3. Coaster: Rounds 1-3

Round 1: MR, SC 6

Round 2: INC around (12)

Round 3: (SC, INC) x6 (18)

3. Coaster: Rounds 4-5

Round 4: (SC 2, INC) x6 (24)

Round 5: (SC 3, INC) x6 (30)

3. Coaster: Complete

Fasten off and weave in ends neatly.

Tip: Use cotton yarn so your completed coaster absorbs liquid well!

These three projects help you practice the most important skills in crochet: working in rounds, increasing, decreasing, changing colors, and finishing your work neatly.

Once you're comfortable with these, you'll be ready for bigger projects like plushies, blankets, and clothing!
